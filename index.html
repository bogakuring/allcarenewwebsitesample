    Tentang Kami

    Klinik gigi pertama sebelum berdirinya All Care Dental Centre didirikan oleh Drg. Gunawan Atmadja, Sp.Kg, MDSc, FICD seorang dokter gigi spesialis restoratif yang memulai profesinya sebagai dokter gigi pada tahun 1969 di Jatinegara Timur, Jakarta.

    Pada tanggal 25 Maret 1990 Dr Gunawan membuka cabang klinik gigi lainnya di Rumah Sakit Mitra Keluarga Jatinegara,

    Sekarang menjadi Mitra International Hospital yang kemudian berafiliasi dengan Health is Care dan meraih ISO 9001. Pada bulan Desember 1998, RS Internasional Bintaro (dengan afiliasi Australia Health Care dan ISO 9001) drg. Gunawan Atmadja dan drg Katharina Atmadja mendirikan All Care Dental Centre.

    Visi dan misi All Care Dental Centre adalah memberikan perawatan gigi sebagai bagian dari kesehatan tubuh secara menyeluruh. Akhirnya pada tahun 2003, karena permintaan yang tinggi, All Care Dental Centre membuka klinik gigi lainnya di daerah Pondok Indah yang berlokasi strategis di seberang Pondok Indah Mall, Jakarta Selatan.

    Layanan

    Visi dan misi All Care Dental Centre adalah memberikan perawatan gigi sebagai bagian dari kesehatan tubuh secara menyeluruh. Berikut ini adalah layanan yang disediakan oleh All Care Dental Centre:

    Preventive

    Kedokteran gigi preventif berkaitan dengan pemeliharaan mekanisme pengunyahan normal dengan memperkuat struktur rongga mulut terhadap kerusakan dan penyakit.

    Cosmetic Dentistry

    Kedokteran gigi kosmetik mencakup berbagai perawatan gigi yang ditujukan untuk memperbaiki penampilan gigi.

    Endodontik (Perawatan Saluran Akar)

    Cabang kedokteran gigi yang menangani penyebab, diagnosis, pencegahan, dan perawatan penyakit pulpa gigi, biasanya dengan membuang saraf dan jaringan lain dari rongga pulpa dan menggantinya dengan bahan tambalan yang sesuai; terapi saluran pulpa; terapi saluran akar.

    Perawatan Pedodontik

    Ini adalah cabang dokter gigi yang merawat gigi anak sejak bayi dan selama kehamilan. Bagi ibu hamil disarankan untuk berkonsultasi dengan dokter gigi spesialis anak untuk mengetahui tentang nutrisi makanan yang dapat dikonsumsi olehnya untuk kesehatan gigi bayi di masa mendatang dan cara membersihkan gusi dan gigi untuk bayi yang baru lahir. Juga karena perubahan hormon selama masa kehamilan, banyak perubahan yang terjadi di dalam mulut ibu, dan sangat penting untuk berkonsultasi dengan dokter gigi Anda secara rutin. Penting juga untuk mulai membawa anak-anak Anda sejak usia tujuh tahun untuk mengunjungi dokter gigi spesialis anak. Ini karena perawatan pencegahan jauh lebih baik dan lebih murah daripada memperbaiki gigi yang sudah rusak.

    Kedokteran Gigi Restoratif

    Kedokteran gigi restoratif adalah studi, diagnosis, dan manajemen terpadu penyakit gigi dan struktur pendukungnya, serta rehabilitasi gigi sesuai kebutuhan fungsional dan estetika individu. Kedokteran gigi restoratif mencakup spesialisasi gigi endodontik, periodontik, dan prostodontik, dan fondasinya didasarkan pada bagaimana hal-hal ini berinteraksi dalam kasus-kasus yang memerlukan perawatan multifaset.

    Perawatan Prostodontik

    Prostodontik, juga dikenal sebagai prostetik gigi atau kedokteran gigi prostetik, adalah satu dari sembilan spesialisasi kedokteran gigi yang diakui oleh American Dental Association, Royal College of Dentists of Canada, dan Royal Australian College of Dental Surgeons. Prostodontik adalah spesialisasi kedokteran gigi yang berkaitan dengan diagnosis, perencanaan perawatan, rehabilitasi, dan pemeliharaan fungsi, kenyamanan, penampilan, dan kesehatan mulut pasien dengan kondisi klinis yang terkait dengan gigi yang hilang atau tidak berfungsi dan/atau jaringan mulut dan maksilofasial menggunakan pengganti yang biokompatibel.

    Perawatan Ortodontik

    Ortodontik, yang sebelumnya disebut ortodontia, adalah spesialisasi pertama kedokteran gigi yang berkaitan dengan studi dan perawatan maloklusi (gigitan yang tidak tepat), yang mungkin disebabkan oleh ketidakteraturan gigi, hubungan rahang yang tidak proporsional, atau keduanya. Perawatan ortodontik dapat difokuskan pada perpindahan gigi saja, atau dapat menangani kontrol dan modifikasi pertumbuhan wajah. Dalam kasus terakhir, lebih baik didefinisikan sebagai "ortopedi dentofasial". Perawatan ortodontik dapat dilakukan untuk alasan estetika semata-mata terkait dengan perbaikan penampilan umum gigi pasien. Namun, ada dokter gigi ortodontik yang bekerja untuk merekonstruksi seluruh wajah daripada berfokus secara eksklusif pada gigi.

    Bedah Mulut

    Bedah Mulut merupakan spesialisasi kedokteran gigi yang diakui secara internasional. Bedah mulut mencakup diagnosis, pembedahan, dan perawatan terkait penyakit, cedera, dan cacat yang melibatkan aspek fungsional dan estetika jaringan keras dan lunak pada kepala, mulut, gigi, gusi, rahang, dan leher. Bedah mulut meliputi, tetapi tidak terbatas pada: implan gigi, pencabutan gigi bungsu, gangguan TMJ, trauma wajah, operasi perbaikan rahang, patologi mulut, anestesi, dan cangkok tulang.

Kondisi ini sering disebut sebagai Karies Gigi Akibat Botol Susu Bayi atau Karies Gigi Anak Usia Dini (gigi berlubang). Dalam beberapa kasus yang tidak diharapkan, bayi dan balita mengalami pembusukan gigi parah yang mengakibatkan gigi harus direstorasi atau dicabut. Kabar baiknya adalah pembusukan dapat dicegah.

      Penyebab gigi berlubang Lapisan tipis bakteri yang lengket dan tidak berwarna yang disebut plak melapisi gigi. Bakteri tersebut menghasilkan asam yang dapat menyerang email gigi. Bila makanan atau cairan manis tetap berada di dalam mulut setelah makan, bakteri memiliki lebih banyak waktu untuk menghasilkan asam penyebab gigi berlubang.

      Apa itu Karies Gigi Akibat Botol Susu Bayi Karies gigi akibat botol susu bayi adalah kondisi yang dapat merusak gigi primer bayi atau anak kecil. Kondisi ini terjadi ketika gigi anak sering terpapar cairan manis dalam jangka waktu lama. Cairan ini termasuk susu (bahkan ASI), susu formula, jus buah, dan cairan manis lainnya.

      Gigi susu hanyalah gigi susu, bukan? Manusia, seperti kebanyakan mamalia, memiliki dua set gigi: gigi primer (gigi bayi) dan gigi permanen (gigi dewasa). Sebagian orang menganggap gigi primer tidak penting karena pada akhirnya akan tanggal untuk memberi ruang bagi gigi dewasa. Sebaliknya, gigi primer "menyimpan" ruang di rahang untuk gigi permanen. Gigi primer membantu anak mengunyah, yang penting untuk pola makan yang sehat. Gigi primer membantu anak belajar mengucapkan kata-kata dan memberi struktur pada wajah.

      Pada saat seorang anak lahir, 20 gigi primer biasanya telah terbentuk di dalam gusi. Empat gigi depan biasanya mulai muncul saat bayi berusia antara enam bulan hingga satu tahun. Sebagian besar anak memiliki 20 gigi primer saat mereka berusia tiga tahun.

      Sayangnya, kerusakan gigi dapat terjadi segera setelah gigi tumbuh. Gigi depan atas bayi biasanya yang terpengaruh, tetapi kerusakan gigi juga dapat merusak gigi lainnya.

      Kerusakan pada gigi primer dapat menyebabkan rasa sakit dan dapat merusak gigi permanen yang masih tumbuh di dalam gusi. Meskipun gigi tersebut tidak terlihat hingga anak berusia sekitar enam tahun, gigi permanen mulai membentuk email gigi sejak tiga hingga empat bulan setelah lahir. Penyakit dapat menyebar ke gigi permanen yang tersembunyi. Kerusakan tersebut dapat dikaitkan dengan masalah kesehatan umum pada beberapa anak.

      Jika gigi primer tanggal sebelum waktunya, gigi dewasa yang baru tumbuh dapat bergeser, sehingga gigitannya tidak teratur dan memerlukan perawatan tambahan. Jika gigi primer tetap sehat hingga siap tanggal dengan sendirinya, ada kemungkinan gigi dewasa tumbuh dengan posisi yang normal.

      Apa yang dapat saya lakukan untuk menjaga kesehatan gigi bayi? Bayi bergantung pada orang tua atau pengasuh untuk kesehatan yang baik. Ambil peran aktif dalam merawat gigi bayi dengan membersihkannya di rumah, menyediakan makanan yang seimbang, dan menjadwalkan kunjungan ke dokter gigi secara teratur.

      Jangan pernah biarkan bayi atau balita tertidur dengan botol yang berisi susu, susu formula, jus buah, cairan manis, atau dot yang dicelupkan ke dalam gula atau madu. Bahkan minuman manis yang diencerkan dapat merusak. Bayi harus menghabiskan susu botol sebelum tidur atau tidur siang (atau menyusui) sebelum tidur.

      Mulailah perawatan mulut sejak dini. Bersihkan gusi bayi dengan kain kasa bersih setelah setiap kali menyusu. Mulailah menyikat gigi anak Anda dengan air segera setelah gigi pertama muncul. Jika Anda mempertimbangkan untuk menggunakan pasta gigi berfluorida sebelum anak Anda berusia dua tahun, tanyakan kepada dokter gigi atau dokter Anda terlebih dahulu. Saat memilih produk perawatan mulut, carilah produk yang memiliki Seal of Acceptance dari American Dental Association.

      Untuk mengurangi risiko kerusakan gigi, anak-anak harus didorong untuk minum dari cangkir sejak ulang tahun pertama mereka.

      Bicaralah dengan dokter gigi Anda tentang penjadwalan kunjungan pertama anak. Sebaiknya kunjungan pertama dilakukan dalam waktu enam bulan sejak gigi pertama tumbuh dan paling lambat pada ulang tahun pertama bayi. Anggap kunjungan pertama anak Anda sebagai "pemeriksaan kesehatan bayi" untuk giginya.

      Selama kunjungan, dokter gigi Anda dapat menunjukkan cara membersihkan gigi anak dengan benar. Selain memeriksa kerusakan gigi dan kondisi lain seperti kebiasaan mengisap jempol, dokter gigi Anda dapat menjelaskan fluorida dan jumlah yang tepat yang dibutuhkan anak untuk gigi yang sehat. Dokter gigi juga dapat merekomendasikan produk perawatan mulut untuk keluarga Anda.

      Ialam banyak kasus, orang tua mengandalkan orang lain untuk merawat bayi setidaknya sebagian waktu. Pastikan untuk berbagi informasi tentang pencegahan kerusakan gigi karena botol susu bayi dengan pengasuh. Menjaga senyum bayi tetap sehat merupakan upaya bersama.

    • Children Dental Care

      As our daily lives seem busier and fasterpaced than ever, one phrase comes to mind: "The future is now. A child’s oral healthcare habits today will have an impact on his health as an adult."Â 
      The good news is that preventive dental care has dramatically improved the oral health of many American children. Nowadays, it’s possible for children to reach adulthood without ever experiencing tooth decay. However, tooth decay remains a serious public health problem: it still is the most common chronic childhood disease that will not resolve without treatment â€“ surpassing asthma by five times. Here are some simple steps you can take to help your child enjoy a lifetime of beautiful smiles.Â 

      Importance of a Balanced DietÂ 
      A balanced diet is critical for the proper development of an unborn child. Teeth begin to form in the second month of pregnancy and to calcify between the third and sixth months of pregnancy. A balanced diet that provides adequate amounts of vitamins A, C and D, protein, calcium and phosphorous helps develop healthy teeth, while poor nutrition can be associated with early childhood caries (tooth decay).Â 

      Keeping Teeth and Gums HealthyÂ 
      Pregnant women may have the desire to eat more frequently between meals. While this is normal, frequent snacking can be an invitation to tooth decay. The decay process begins with plaque, an invisible, sticky layer of harmful bacteria that constantly forms on teeth. The bacteria convert sugar and starches that remain in the mouth to an acid that attacks tooth enamel. Brushing your teeth twice a day and cleaning between teeth daily with floss or another interdental cleaner can reduce the risk of decay. A mother’s caries-causing bacteria can be transmitted to her child, so it is important to reduce plaque and have teeth free of decay before the birth of a child.Â 

      Plaque that is not removed can irritate the gums, making them red, tender, and likely to bleed easily. This condition is called gingivitis and can lead to more serious periodontal disease that affects the gums and bone that anchor teeth in place. During pregnancy, a woman’s hormone levels rise considerably. Gingivitis, especially common during the second to eighth months of pregnancy, may cause red, puffy or tender gums that tend to bleed when brushed. This sensitivity is an exaggerated response to plaque and is caused by an increased level of progesterone. Poor periodontal health in the mother can lead to adverse pregnancy outcomes like premature delivery and low birth weight of the baby. Mothers should see a dentist regularly throughout pregnancy. The dentist may recommend more frequent cleanings during the second trimester or early third trimester to help avoid problems.Â 

      THE GROWING YEARS: FROM BIRTH TO SIXÂ 
      Baby’s First TeethÂ 
      People usually think of a newborn baby as having no teeth. But the 20 primary teeth that will erupt in the next three years already are present at birth in the baby’s jawbones. At birth, most of the crowns of the primary teeth are almost complete, and the chewing surfaces of the permanent molars have begun forming.Â 

      Primary teeth are important in normal development â€” for chewing, speaking, and appearance. In addition, the primary teeth hold the space in the jaws for the permanent teeth. Both primary and permanent teeth help give the face its shape and form.Â 

      A baby’s front four teeth usually erupt first, typically at about six months of age, although some children don’t have their first tooth until 12 or 14 months. Most children have a full set of 20 primary teeth by the time they are three years old. As your child grows, the jaws also grow, making room for the permanent teeth that will begin to erupt at about age six. At the same time, the roots of the primary teeth begin to be absorbed by the tissues around them, and the permanent teeth under them begin to erupt. Typically, most children have the majority of their permanent teeth by 12 to 14 years of age. The remaining four permanent molars, often called “wisdom teeth,” erupt around age 21 to complete the set of 32 permanent teeth.Â 

      When teeth begin erupting, some babies may have sore or tender gums. Gently rubbing your child’s gums with a clean finger or a wet gauze pad can be soothing. You also can give the baby a clean teething ring to chew on, but never dipped in sugar or syrup. If your child is still cranky and uncomfortable, consult your dentist or physician. Contrary to common belief, fever is not normal for a teething baby. If your infant has an unusually high or persistent fever while teething, call your physician.Â 

      Cleaning Your Child’s TeethÂ 
      Begin cleaning the baby’s mouth during the first few days after birth. After every feeding, wipe the baby’s gums either with a clean, wet gauze pad or with a washcloth or towel. This removes residual food that can harm erupting teeth, and helps the child grow accustomed to having his mouth checked.Â 

      When your child’s teeth begin to erupt, brush them gently with a child’s size soft toothbrush and water. Brush the teeth of children over age two with a pea-sized amount of fluoride toothpaste. (Consult with your child’s dentist or physician if you are considering using fluoride toothpaste before age two). To clean your child’s teeth, you can sit with the child’s head in your lap. Be sure you can see into the child’s mouth easily. Check your child’s toothbrush often and replace it when it is worn. Bent or frayed bristles will not remove plaque effectively.Â 

      First Dental VisitÂ 
      As your child’s first tooth erupts, consult with your child’s dentist regarding scheduling your child’s first visit. It is advantageous for the first dental visit to occur within six months after the first tooth erupts, but not later than the first birthday. Treat the first dental visit as you would a well baby checkup with the child’s physician.Â 

      During the first visit, the dentist will check: (1) for cavities; (2) to see how well the teeth are being cleaned and offer cleaning suggestions if necessary; (3) to make sure the proper number of teeth have erupted; and (4) to see that the child is receiving the proper amount of fluoride because too much or too little can lead to problems. The dentist also will answer any questions the parents may have. By starting visits at an early age, you will help your child build a lifetime of good dental habits.Â 

      Preventing Decay of Primary TeethÂ 
      As soon as teeth appear in the mouth, decay can occur. One of the risk factors for early childhood caries (sometimes called baby bottle tooth decay) is frequent and prolonged exposure of a baby’s teeth to liquids containing sugar, including milk, breast milk, formula and fruit juice. Tooth decay can occur when parents or caregivers put a baby to bed with a bottle â€“ or use milk, formula or juice as a pacifier for a fussy babyÂ 

      If you use a pacifier, use a clean one. Never dip a pacifier in sugar or honey before giving it to a baby. Prolonged use of pacifiers can harm the teeth just like prolonged thumb sucking, but it often is easier to wean a child from a pacifier than a thumb. Encourage children to drink from a cup by their first birthday, and discourage frequent use of a training cup. Never allow a baby to take a bottle to bed at night or naptime.Â 

      “Holding Space Open” for Primary TeethÂ 
      Sometimes a primary tooth is lost before the permanent tooth beneath it is ready to erupt. If a primary tooth is lost too early, nearby teeth can tip or shift into the vacant space. When the permanent tooth is ready to emerge into the space, there may not be enough room for it. The new tooth may be unable to emerge â€“ or may emerge in an abnormal position. If your child loses a tooth prematurely, the dentist may recommend a space maintainer, a plastic or metal appliance that holds open the space left by the missing tooth. The dentist will remove this device once the permanent tooth begins to erupt.Â 

      ThumbsuckingÂ 
      Sucking is a natural reflex and infants and young children may suck on thumbs, fingers, pacifiers and other objects. It may make them feel safe and happy or provide a sense of security at difficult periods. Since thumbsucking is relaxing, it may induce sleep.Â 

      Thumbsucking that persists beyond the eruption of the permanent teeth can cause problems with tooth alignment and the proper growth of the mouth. How intensely a child sucks on fingers or thumbs will determine whether or not dental problems may result. Children who rest their thumbs passively in their mouths are less likely to have difficulty than those who vigorously suck their thumbs.Â 

      Children should cease thumbsucking by the time their permanent front teeth are ready to erupt. Usually, children stop between the ages of two and four. Peer pressure causes many school-aged children to stop. If you have concerns about thumbsucking or use of a pacifier, consult your dentist.Â 

      Toothbrushing for ChildrenÂ 
      Brush and floss your child’s teeth until he or she is at least six years old. By age six or seven, children should be able to brush their own teeth twice a day â€” with supervision until about age 10 or 11, to make sure they are doing a thorough job. However, each child is different. Your dentist can help you determine whether the child has the skill level to brush properly.Â 

      Choose a child-size toothbrush for ease of use. The variety of colors and designs can provide extra fun and motivation to keep children brushing. There also are powered or mechanical brushes available for children, so ask your dentist if one is right for your childÂ 

      Proper brushing removes plaque from the inner, outer and chewing surfaces. When teaching how to brush, you may wish to stand behind the child and hold the brush to be certain that brushing is done properly.Â 
      Focus on FluorideÂ 
      Fluoride is vital for strong, decay-resistant teeth. Fluoride is one of the most effective agents for preventing tooth decay. It combines with tooth enamel to strengthen it against decay. Fluoride may also reverse microscopic cavities by enhancing the process in which minerals, including calcium, are incorporated into the teeth.Â 

      The most effective way for your child to get fluoride’s protection is by drinking water containing the right amount of the mineral, (about one part fluoride per million parts water). This is of special benefit to children, because fluoride strengthens teeth as they form. Children who from birth drink water containing fluoride have up to 50 percent fewer cavities. Many of them remain cavity free through their teens.Â 

      Before you give your child any vitamin or supplement that contains fluoride, check with your dentist to see if one is needed. Discuss your child’s fluoride needs with your dentist or pediatrician. Based on your dentist’s assessment of your family’s oral health, the use of additional fluoride-containing products may or may not be recommended.Â 

      THE GROWING YEARS: FROM SIX TO 12Â 
      As children develop, their jaw and face continue to change. The transition from baby teeth to adult teeth is gradual. By the time they reach adulthood, most children will progress from their 20 primary teeth to 32 permanent (adult) teeth. All the while, the jaw gradually expands to make room for the additional 12 teeth.Â 
      At about age six, although it may start earlier, children begin to shed (lose) their front teeth on top and bottom. During the next six or so years, permanent teeth gradually will replace the primary teeth.Â 
      The first permanent molars usually erupt between ages five and six. For that reason, they are often called the six-year molars. They are among the “extra” permanent teeth in that they don’t replace an existing baby tooth. These important adult teeth are often mistaken for baby teeth.       The Goal: Preserving The ToothÂ 
      In the past, injured or diseased teeth frequently had to be removed. Today, they often can be saved through endodontic treatment.       Pulp particularÂ 
      A root canal treatment generally involves the removal and replacement of a tooth’s pulp. The pulp is soft tissue containing blood vessels, nerves and connective tissue.

      • The pulp is found in a canal that runs through the center of the hard tissue on the inside of the tooth (the dentin).
      • The pulp extends from the pulp chamber in the crown down through the root canal to the tip of the root in the jawbone.
      • A tooth has only one pulp chamber but may have more than one root and several root canals.
      If pulp becomes damaged through injury or disease and cannot repair itself, bacteria and their products can leak into the pulp and cause the pulp to die.       • Local anesthetic usually is given, to maintain patient comfort.
      • The affected tooth is isolated from saliva with a rubberlike sheet called a dam.
      • An opening is made through the crown of the tooth. The pulp is removed, and then the root is cleaned and shaped. Medication may be added to the pulp chamber and root canal(s) to help eliminate bacteria.
      • A temporary filling is placed in the crown opening to keep saliva out. Antibiotics may be prescribed if an infection is present and has spread beyond the end of the root(s).

      • The temporary filling is removed.
      • The root canal is filled and permanently sealed. (A metal or plastic rod or post may be placed in the root canal for structural support.)
